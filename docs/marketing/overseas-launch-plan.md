# Tuziyo Overseas Launch Plan

Last updated: 2026-07-16

## 1. Launch objective

Establish Tuziyo as a credible multi-model AI image studio, acquire the first cohort of activated overseas users, earn relevant product and editorial links, and learn which audience-message pair produces repeat usage.

Primary 30-day targets:

- 1,000 qualified website visits
- 150 new registrations
- 60 users who complete at least one generation
- 20 users who return within seven days
- 15 accepted directory or community listings
- 5 editorial, creator, or partner backlinks
- 10 substantive user conversations or feedback threads

## 2. Positioning

Core promise:

> Generate, compare, and finish AI images in one workflow.

Launch offer:

> Every new account receives 10 free credits. No credit card required.

Primary audiences:

1. Designers and creative technologists comparing model behavior.
2. Indie founders and marketers producing launch, product, and social assets.
3. AI creators who currently jump between several model websites.
4. Creators who need practical crop, resize, convert, and inpainting tools after generation.

Reasons to believe:

- Multiple model families are available from one studio.
- Model guide pages document current controls and credit costs.
- Sessions preserve prompts, references, settings, and results.
- Browser finishing tools reduce workflow switching.
- Free credits lower the cost of the first successful result.

## 3. Channel strategy

### Tier 1: High-intent launch communities

Product Hunt:

- Prepare a complete launch rather than publishing immediately with partial assets.
- Launch at 12:01 a.m. Pacific when the maker can remain active for the full day.
- Ask supporters to visit and leave useful feedback; never ask directly for upvotes.
- Use the maker comment to explain the workflow problem, why Tuziyo is different, and what feedback is most valuable.

Hacker News:

- Use `Show HN: Tuziyo – Compare multiple AI image models in one studio`.
- Lead with the working product and technical/product decisions, not marketing claims.
- Mention the open-source repository, Cloudflare/React Router stack, local browser tools, and the clear privacy boundary between local tools and cloud generation.
- Be available to answer technical questions immediately after posting.

Reddit:

- Post one community-specific story at a time; do not mass-post identical links.
- Best initial communities: r/SideProject, r/indiehackers, r/SaaS, r/ArtificialInteligence, and model-specific communities only when their rules permit self-promotion.
- Use a feedback request or build story, disclose that the poster built the product, and explain the free-credit offer after the product value.
- Spend the following week contributing non-promotional answers and sharing useful model-comparison findings.

Indie Hackers / Peerlist:

- Publish a build story centered on consolidating a fragmented creative workflow.
- Share real activation and retention lessons after the first week.
- Launch on Peerlist Launchpad with the same visual kit and a shorter founder narrative.

### Tier 2: Discovery directories and durable citations

Priority free/freemium targets:

- BetaList
- Uneed
- MicroLaunch
- Peerlist Launchpad
- DevHunt, if its current categories accept this product
- AIToolsDirectory
- Aixploria
- AI Tool Hunt

Paid directories should only be tested after at least one free channel demonstrates registration conversion. Do not buy bulk low-quality backlink packages.

### Tier 3: Compounding content and search

Publish one high-intent page or article each week:

1. Nano Banana vs Nano Banana Pro vs Nano Banana 2
2. Seedream 5 Pro vs GPT Image 2 for campaign visuals
3. Best AI image model for product photography
4. How to keep character or product consistency with reference images
5. AI image credit cost comparison inside Tuziyo
6. Prompt examples for product shots, posters, and social ads

Every article should include:

- A concrete comparison or workflow
- Original screenshots or generated examples
- A direct link to the relevant model guide
- A free-credit call to action
- A short factual summary suitable for AI search systems

### Tier 4: Creator and partner outreach

Build a list of 30 small, relevant creators rather than targeting large generic AI accounts:

- YouTube creators reviewing image models
- Newsletter writers covering generative design
- Prompt educators
- Product photography and ecommerce creators
- Indie maker communities

Offer:

- A transparent free-credit code or manually granted evaluation credits
- Side-by-side model examples for their audience
- A co-created comparison article or video
- No requirement for positive coverage

## 4. Four-week execution calendar

### Week 1: Foundation and soft launch

- Align homepage, README, repository metadata, blog, and directory copy.
- Publish the launch article.
- Add `llms.txt` and verify sitemaps, canonical tags, structured data, and social previews.
- Submit to 5-8 free directories.
- Publish one Reddit post in the best-fit community.
- Collect activation events: landing view, register, first generation, second generation, and return visit.

### Week 2: Community launch

- Publish Show HN and remain available for replies.
- Publish the Indie Hackers build story.
- Submit to the next 5-8 directories.
- Start 10 personalized creator outreach conversations.
- Publish the first model comparison article.

### Week 3: Product Hunt

- Schedule Product Hunt only after visuals, maker comment, demo, and reply coverage are ready.
- Coordinate launch-day posts on X, LinkedIn, Indie Hackers, and one relevant Reddit thread without asking for upvotes.
- Reply to every meaningful comment.
- Record traffic, registration, activation, and credit usage by source.

### Week 4: Evidence-led iteration

- Publish a transparent launch retrospective with actual results.
- Double down on the two best converting messages/channels.
- Remove channels that send traffic without activation.
- Contact relevant writers with the strongest comparison data or user examples.
- Prepare a meaningful future Product Hunt relaunch only for a significant product iteration.

## 5. Measurement

Use UTM parameters consistently:

`utm_source`, `utm_medium`, `utm_campaign=launch_2026_07`, and `utm_content`.

Core funnel:

1. Landing session
2. AI Studio opened
3. Registration started
4. Registration completed
5. First generation started
6. First generation completed
7. Second generation completed
8. Seven-day return
9. Pricing viewed
10. Purchase completed

Weekly decision rules:

- Keep channels with at least 10 qualified visits and above-average activation.
- Rewrite channels with clicks but weak registration.
- Fix onboarding if registration is healthy but first generation is weak.
- Improve model selection and prompt examples if first generation succeeds but second generation is weak.
- Do not judge directories on referral traffic alone; track indexed brand mentions and relevant backlinks.

## 6. Link-building rules

Prioritize:

- Relevant product profiles
- Real founder profiles
- Editorial comparisons
- Partner tutorials
- Open-source references
- Creator reviews
- Community threads with substantive discussion

Avoid:

- Automated comment spam
- Identical Reddit posts across communities
- Purchased bulk links
- Irrelevant guest-post farms
- Claims that all Tuziyo processing is local
- Asking Product Hunt users directly for upvotes
